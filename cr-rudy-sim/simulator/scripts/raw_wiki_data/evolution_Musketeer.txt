## Release Date

The Musketeer's Evolution is aRarecardthat is unlocked with 6 Evolution Shards. It spawns a Musketeer, with identical stats to the original. After deployment, she will gain 3 infinite-range attacks that will target the closest troop or building from anywhere in the Arena in front of her, dealing 1.8 times the damage of her normal attacks. The Musketeer takes the appearance of a woman who has purple eyes, coiffed curly hair with a ponytail, eyebrows and lipstick, and wears a royal blue tunic with a purple cloth on it, a purple cape with aCard Evolutionsymbol in the center and gloves, a metal helmet with a team-colored feather and a tiny crater on it, a dark brown belt and boots with dark purple straps, while carrying a blunderbuss-styled musket as her weapon with dark gray spheres as her ammunition and a scope on top. The Musketeer's Evolution costs 4Elixirand requires 2 cycles to deploy.

## Strategy[]

  - The Evolved Musketeer can never targetCrown Towerswith her sniper snots. This allows her to attacktroopsandbuildingsbehind them, such as anElixir Collector.
  - The Evolved Musketeer will only expend her sniper shots when she is not in range of her regular attacks. To ensure that she uses them, place her from a distance from her intended target.
  - The full 3 sniper shots can defeat any troop with as many hitpoints as aBaby Dragon. Two of them are enough to defeat any troop with as many hitpoints asElixir Golemites, while a single sniper snot can eliminate anything with as many hitpoints as Elixir Blobs.
  - The Evolved Musketeer is a hard counter to theEvolved Wizard. With her first sniper shot, she will destroy the Evolved Wizard's shield, while her two other shots can defeat him on their own. This can counter him for a +1Elixirtrade, while she has full hitpoints for a counterattack.

## Trivia[]

  - Themusketis a muzzle-loaded long gun that appeared as a smoothbore weapon in the early 16th century, at first as a heavier variant of thearquebus, capable of penetratingplate armour. By the mid-16th century, this type of musket gradually disappeared as the use of heavy armour declined, but muskets continued as the generic term for smoothbore long guns until the mid-19th century. In turn, this style of musket was retired in the 19th century whenriflesbecame common, which were more accurate than smoothbore muskets and could inflict more severe injuries.In the Arabic and Persian language settings of the game, the Musketeer's name translates to "Knight" and "Riflewoman" respectively.
