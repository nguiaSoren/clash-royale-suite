## Release Date

The Skeleton Barrel's Evolution is aCommoncardunlocked with 6 Evolution Shards. It spawns a Skeleton Barrel with hitpoints 25% higher and death damage 64% higher than the original. It spawns with 2 barrels, each with 7Skeletons: one dropped when it reaches 75% hitpoints, and another dropped when it is defeated. It is a golden-spiked, dark brown, double barrel with aCard Evolutionsymbol on both sides chained to a pair of team-colored balloons with a violet skull printed on them and a small spike on top of them. The Skeleton Barrel's Evolution costs 3Elixirand requires 2 cycles to deploy.

## Strategy[]

  - Due to the addition of a second barrel, as well as the first one dropping early, the Evolved Skeleton Barrel becomes far more dangerous. If the opponent carries only a smallspellto take the card down, the opponent must either use it on the first barrel'sSkeletonsand the remaining barrel, which risks chip damage from the second barrel's skeletons, or wait for the surviving barrel to drop the Skeletons and defeat them all with the spell, risking chip damage from both the remaining barrel and the Skeletons from the first barrel. From this interaction, some things can be noted.This is valid forTower Princess,Cannoneer, andRoyal Chef, who will quickly trigger the Evolved Skeleton Barrel's first barrel drop, but let the second one connect. ForDagger Duchess, she will defeat the Barrel quickly enough so that all of the Skeletons clump up and the small spell will make quick work of them.A well timedGiant Snowballwill avoid a lot of said damage, and anEvolved Giant Snowballprevents almost all damage, all for a +1Elixirtrade. The same can be applied to theTornadofor a neutral Elixir trade.A 2-Elixir spell + a Spirit card can prevent all damage when properly timed. AnElectro Spiritcan defeat the Skeletons from the first barrel and stun and damage the remaining barrel, leaving the spell for the second barrel, while, for the other Spirits, it's best to let the spell damage the remaining barrel + the first barrel's Skeletons, while the Spirit card defeats the remaining Skeletons.A well timedEarthquakewill prevent all damage done by Skeletons, only allowing the second barrel's death damage. For Dagger Duchess, it completely prevents damage for a neutral Elixir trade.
  - Since a singular Evolved Skeleton Barrel's death damage is slightly more than a normal Skeleton Barrel, their combined death damage deals more damage than aRocket. As such, it can be good to prepare a big push or a counterpush with it, so that the Evolved Skeleton Barrel can connect both of its barrels on theCrown Towerwhile the other cards take hits againstbuildingsand anti-air units. If the Skeletons are not dealt with, then a Crown Tower is lost, and even if the death Skeletons do not connect, the Crown Tower will have suffered a lot of damage.
  - The Evolved Skeleton Barrel is also a great defensive card. Against clumped up units with some anti-air cards, one can deploy the Evolved Skeleton Barrel on top of them to let itself drop one or both barrels on the units. A single barrel's death damage can defeat up toMinions, and the combined death damage can defeatElixir Blobs. The death Skeletons may also be useful for extra damage, finishing off certain units that survive with a silver of health, or extra distraction.
  - To combat the Evolved Skeleton Barrel, anti-air units with a high hit speed or an AoE attack should help. When put at a distance so that the death damage doesn't take them out, troops who attack quickly, likeArchers,Musketeer,Phoenix,Little Prince, orDart Goblinwill quickly deplete the barrels' HP and assist in defeating the Skeletons one by one, while AoE troops, likeFirecracker,Wizard,Princess, andExecutioner, will clear the death Skeletons all at once while dealing with the barrel.
  - Splash troops who do not target air, likeValkyrie,Skeleton King,Bomber,Dark Prince,Goblin Demolisher, andBowler, can still work to take the Skeletons down, but it leaves the Crown Tower vulnerable to one barrel's death damage (or both of them).
  - A well-timedEvolved Furnacecan be sufficient to counter all but the second barrel's death damage (or even counter all damage) when the Evolved Skeleton Barrel takes the Crown Tower damage. One must place it so that the firstFire Spiritspawned clears the first barrel's Skeletons. Then, once it attacks, the second Fire Spirit might spawn just as the second barrel deploys more Skeletons.
  - TheBomb Toweris the most reliable counter to an Evolved Skeleton Barrel for -1 Elixir. It's tanky enough to handle the barrel's death damage and the Skeletons' own damage, while having an AoE attack that quickly dispatches the card's death spawns. It can also survive an Earthquake with high enough health to still counter the Evolved Skeleton Barrel.
  - When timed correctly, theEvolved Cannonand theEvolved Teslacan clear out the first barrel's Skeletons with their abilities, cripple the second barrel, and deal with said barrel's Skeletons. The former is a neutral elixir trade, while the latter can counter the Evolved Skeleton Barrel well even with just a normal center placement.
  - This is valid forTower Princess,Cannoneer, andRoyal Chef, who will quickly trigger the Evolved Skeleton Barrel's first barrel drop, but let the second one connect. ForDagger Duchess, she will defeat the Barrel quickly enough so that all of the Skeletons clump up and the small spell will make quick work of them.
  - A well timedGiant Snowballwill avoid a lot of said damage, and anEvolved Giant Snowballprevents almost all damage, all for a +1Elixirtrade. The same can be applied to theTornadofor a neutral Elixir trade.
  - A 2-Elixir spell + a Spirit card can prevent all damage when properly timed. AnElectro Spiritcan defeat the Skeletons from the first barrel and stun and damage the remaining barrel, leaving the spell for the second barrel, while, for the other Spirits, it's best to let the spell damage the remaining barrel + the first barrel's Skeletons, while the Spirit card defeats the remaining Skeletons.
  - A well timedEarthquakewill prevent all damage done by Skeletons, only allowing the second barrel's death damage. For Dagger Duchess, it completely prevents damage for a neutral Elixir trade.

## Trivia[]

  - The word "Skeleton" comes from the Greek "σκελετός" (skeletós), meaning dried up or withered.
  - The Skeleton Barrel is 1 of 6 evolvedcardsassociated withSkeletons, with the others beingBomber,Witch,Wall Breakers,Skeleton Army, and of course,Skeletons.
  - The Skeleton Barrel is 1 of 4 evolvedtroopsto die as a part of their attack, the others being theIce Spirit, Wall Breakers, and Runners.
  - The barrel of the Evolved Skeleton Barrel looks similar to the hull of theRocket.
